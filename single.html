Enclaraa — Agentic Legal AI, Built by VenRAAG

Enclaraa is the flagship Legal AI platform I founded and architected under VenRAAG. It brings Agentic Retrieval-Augmented Generation into the day-to-day workflow of law firms, in-house legal teams and compliance functions through a layered architecture: secure on-prem document ingestion, hybrid vector & keyword retrieval, domain-tuned language models, and evaluator-optimiser agents that verify their own outputs before handing them back to the user.

Traditional RAG systems hallucinate on dense legal text because they treat every document the same. Enclaraa is different: we fine-tune retrieval and generation against legal-specific corpora, add citation-level traceability, and apply agentic verification — so every answer, clause or draft ships with provenance the legal team can trust and defend in front of partners, regulators or a court.

Arvya Tech: AI Invoice Extraction & Customised AI

At Arvya Tech, I lead the deployment of AI invoice extraction and customised AI applications for enterprise clients. Our invoice engine combines large language models, computer vision and custom parsers to read multi-format invoices, POs and receipts, extract line-item detail without manual templates, and plug straight into the client's ERP or accounts-payable pipeline.

The result: finance teams stop re-keying data, exception queues shrink, and compliance gets a clean, auditable trail of every document processed. More broadly, Arvya Tech partners with enterprises to identify where customised AI can actually move a KPI — not just generate demos — and then ships production-grade systems on AWS with full CI/CD and observability.

Recognition

Team Enclaraa were crowned winners of the global LegalTechTalk Hackathon powered by QuantumBlack, AI by McKinsey and Cleary Gottlieb — beating teams from leading institutions worldwide by shipping a real-time AI contract assistant under 48-hour pressure. That victory became the launchpad for Enclaraa's commercial release and its recognition among the Top 50 global legal-tech startups.

Alongside this, VenRAAG was awarded the Generator Bursary sponsored by Santander Universities and the University of Greenwich, and selected for the Investment Readiness Programme — validating the commercial potential of the research-led approach behind Enclaraa.
